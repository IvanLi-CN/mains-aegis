# CLM1612P1412 vs SFJ-1412 (datasheet comparison)

This note compares the two parts based on the datasheets stored in this repo:

- PTTC(Polytronics Tech): `docs/datasheets/CLM1612P1412/CLM1612P1412.pdf` / `docs/datasheets/CLM1612P1412/CLM1612P1412.md`
- Dexerials: `docs/datasheets/SFJ-1412/SFJ-1412.pdf` / `docs/datasheets/SFJ-1412/SFJ-1412.md`

## Summary table (key parameters for 4S / 12A parts)

| Item | CLM1612P1412 (PTTC) | SFJ-1412 (Dexerials) |
|---|---|---|
| Part family / function | Current Limiting Module (CLM): fuse element + resistive heating element (can be driven by external overvoltage-detect circuit) | Self Control Protector (SCP): surface mount fuse for Li-ion battery secondary protection (overcurrent / overcharge interruption) |
| Rated current | 12 A | 12 A |
| Cells in series (target) | 4 cells | 4 cells |
| Operating voltage (4 cells) | 10.5 ~ 19.6 V | 10.5 ~ 19.6 V |
| Heater resistance (4 cells) | 11.2 ~ 20.0 Ω | 11.2 ~ 20.0 Ω |
| Fuse resistance | 1.5 ~ 3.5 mΩ | 2.4 mΩ (typ.) |
| Rated voltage / breaking capacity | Vmax 36 VDC; Ibreak 50 A | Rated breaking capacity table for SFJ-xx12 series includes 50 A at 36 VDC (UL) |
| Certifications (as stated) | “us” and “ATOVRheinland” (see electrical specifications table) | UL248-14 (File No. E167588), TUV (Certificate No. J9650637) |

## Practical impact (derived, from Rfuse)

At 12 A, the voltage drop and dissipation on the fuse resistance can be approximated as:

- CLM1612P1412: `V ≈ I·R = 12 A · (1.5~3.5 mΩ) = 18~42 mV`; `P ≈ I²·R = 0.216~0.504 W`
- SFJ-1412: `V ≈ 12 A · 2.4 mΩ = 28.8 mV`; `P ≈ 0.346 W`

## Selection notes

- If you need explicitly stated UL/TUV certification identifiers in the datasheet text, `SFJ-1412` is clearer.
- If you care about minimizing voltage drop / heating at high current, compare the effective `Rfuse` and validate with your PCB thermal conditions (both vendors mention PCB thermal capacity affects characteristics).
